5) TEXT OVERLAY  (do this in Figma/Canva AFTER generating)
----------------------------------------------------------------
AI image tools mangle long text. Generate the artwork WITHOUT text (or with
Ideogram/DALL·E for short words only), then overlay copy in Figma/Canva using:
  • Headline font: Inter, weight 700
  • Body font: Inter, weight 500
Suggested copy:
  Headline:  "AI-Powered Travel & Hotel Booking for WordPress"
  Sub:       "Trips · Hotels · Smart Booking · AI Recommendations"
  Badge:     "NEW" / "Lifetime Deal" (AppSumo) / a 5-star strip (CodeCanyon)
  CTA pill:  "Get the Plugin" / "Get Lifetime Access"
  AI-angle headline variants (use on the AI banners 4b / 4d):
    • "Let AI build your trip pages."
    • "AI writes the trip. You take the booking."
    • "An AI co-pilot for your travel store."
  AI sub:    "AI Trip Builder · AI customer replies · AI concierge · Smart search"
  AI badge:  "✦ AI SUITE" / "Powered by Claude & GPT"
Place text on the clean negative-space side. Keep the logo mark (paper-plane
tile) top-left.

6) NEGATIVE PROMPT  (append where the tool supports it)
----------------------------------------------------------------
--no  gibberish text, misspelled words, watermark, low-res, blurry, jpeg

purple/blue dominant (keep it ORANGE/amber).

----------------------------------------------------------------
7) QUICK TIPS
----------------------------------------------------------------
• Generate at the largest size, then downscale (sharper than upscaling).
• Lock the gradient: always say "#FD4621 to #FF8A3D" so banners stay on-brand.
• Make a 3-variation set per banner, pick the cleanest composition, then add text.
• For CodeCanyon, the 590×300 must look great as a small thumbnail too — keep one
  clear focal point and avoid tiny details.
• Reuse the SAME laptop/UI mockup across AppSumo + CodeCanyon for brand cohesion.
• Tools that handle short baked-in text well: Ideogram, DALL·E 3. Avoid baking
  long sentences anywhere — overlay them yourself.
